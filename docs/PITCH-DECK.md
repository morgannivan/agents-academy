## The Problem

### The AI Agent Market Is Exploding — But Broken

The AI agent economy is projected at **$10.9B in 2026**, yet the ecosystem lacks the infrastructure to make agents trustworthy, discoverable, and tradeable.

**Three critical failures define today's landscape:**

1. **No Capability Verification** — Six major agent marketplaces launched in February 2026 alone (Claude Marketplace, GPT Store expansion, Microsoft Agent Store, Google Agentspace, Salesforce AgentExchange, and OpenClaw Hub). **None of them verify agent capability before listing.** Buyers have no way to know if an agent actually works.

2. **Catastrophic Security Gaps** — **88% of organizations** have experienced AI-related security incidents in the past year. **90% of deployed agents** are over-permissioned, creating massive attack surfaces. Shadow AI adds an average of **$670K in extra breach costs** per incident.

3. **No Quality Standard** — There is no universal certification for agent behavior, safety, or performance. Builders can't prove quality. Buyers can't compare. Enterprises can't adopt with confidence.

**The result:** A multi-billion dollar market where nobody trusts what they're buying.
